Exploding/Analysing Quotes

The Main part

Quote      Location      Language Technique   Why The Poet Used This Quote     
"Prize" First stanza, third line: "they said. ‘You’ve won the top prize," Metaphor      When the "universal lotteries" said that adcock has won the ultra-super global special, the top "prize", she sets the tone of the poem, making it become sort of unexpected. This is a metaphor because it is not actually a prize. Here in this instance, the prize represents the experience to be able to win a lottery. an experience to give "way to" her "emotion" . Adcock used this metaphor beacause experience is valuable and desired, much like prizes. she also used the word "top" to even further demonstrate the value of the prize. The "prize" in the poem is the million pounds that she is about to recieve. But here, the people at universal lotteries creates a sarcastic joke, by putting, "Or, actually with more than a million-. Universal Lotteries also mocked Adcock's innocence by adding"not that it makes a lot of difference once your a millionaire." thereafter, "they laughed." Here, the poet gives us a hint that the prize is fake, as an extra million is actually a major difference in real life.
"Flying Saucer" Last three lines, second stanza: “I feel the top of my head has floated off, out through the window, revolving like a flying saucer.” Simile      The poet compared “the top of her head” to “a flying saucer”. Literally, a “flying saucer” is a plate traveling through the air, but metaphorically it refers to the mixed emotions the person felt at the time. First, she was obviously excited about winning a lottery, but at the same time felt unbelievable that she was so lucky. Greed developed in her mind and made her lost in the illusion of winning a million pounds. She was also confused because she “haven’t bought a lottery ticket for years and years.” This also supports her thought that this was impossible. The image of a “flying saucer” presents the excitement, greed and confusion that the person experienced after hearing she had won the prize.
"Lottery Tickt" Fifth stanza, first line: “Nearly everyone’s bought a ticket in some lottery or another, once at least.” Symbolism      Literally, the “lottery ticket” refers to the ticket information that Universal Lotteries bought up, which was then fed into the computer to “see who the lucky person is.” MEtaphorically, the lottery ticket that the person bought “years and years ago” is a chance for her to win a lottery. Even if she didn’t win an actual tangible prize, the experience of winning a million pounds is also valuable. Therefore symbolically, the lottery ticket connotes the opportunity to gain a special experience, such as winning a lottery. Even a ticket bought a long time ago can provide an opportunity to win an experience as the prize, so things you’ve done a long time ago may be a passage to something unique in the future. Without buying the ticket, the chance of winning the lottery is 0%. To summarize, the lottery ticket symbolizes the opportunity to gain a remarkable experience.

Message of the Poem

The poem is about how emotions could greatly affect oneself, and could even momentarily affect your view of reality, since it took a while for Adcock to realise that this all could be fake, and "universal lotteries were just lying". She also demonstrates our human fascination with luck, and how we- at least once in our life, have wasted money on lottery tickets, hoping to win some sort of prize. Adcock also named the poem "The Telephone Call", and not "A Telephone Call" This is probably because she had an experience similar to this within her life, and the experience might have been memorable, or have possibly changed her life. She also used humor to keep the reader of the poem reading to the end.
